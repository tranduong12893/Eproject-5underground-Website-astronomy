SkyPortal

If on the computer, the most famous stargazing software is Stellarium, for mobile devices such as Android and iOS phones, tablets, SkyPortal is the best application available today.

SkyPortal is built on the platform of SkySafari, a sky simulator application from Simulation Curriculum, a simulation software developer that owns the popular Starry Night sky simulation software for Windows and MacOS computers . SkySafari has 3 versions: SkySafari Basic (0.99 Euro / USD), SkySafari Plus (6.99 Euro / USD), and SkySafari Pro (19.99 Euro / USD). SkyPortal uses the same library with the SkySafari Basic version. That means when you download and install the free SkyPortal application, you are using an application equivalent to both the interface and the library with SkySafari Basic. In other words, you're using the 0.99 Euro / USD SkySafari Basic commercial app for absolutely free!

Inheriting the sky library from SkySafari Basic, SkyPortal has data on 120,000 stars, more than 200 clusters of stars, nebulae, galaxies. and dozens of asteroids, comets, and artificial satellites - including the ISS. All of this is enough for an amateur stargazer to experience the night sky with the most basic of observatories.

The best thing about this free app is that users can automatically control and fine-tune Celestron's computer telescopes from their mobile devices through the StarSense kit and technology. WIFI.

Celestron experts have partnered with SkySafari app developers to provide the best cosmic experience in a free mobile app. SkyPortal is available on both Android and iOS mobile platforms.

Sky simulator

SkyPortal is capable of simulating the night sky and planning the observation program with a customized list of the best objects based on the user's precise time and location. SkyPortal will forecast when the Great Red Spot on Jupiter will appear, mimicking passing phenomena, eclipse events, and other celestial events. SkyPortal provides hundreds of photos and over 4 hours of voice narration to enhance the user experience.

  • Simulate the night sky from anywhere on Earth, from 100 years ago in the past or 100 years in the future.
  • Compass mode (with suitable devices): Hold your mobile device facing the sky to display real-time synchronized celestial objects, from names of stars, constellations, planets, to to the nebulae or galaxies.
  • The control of Celestron's WiFi telescopes matches the complex stand model for precise out-of-tune tuning.
  • Simulate traversing, intercourse, eclipse, and other events with SkyPortal's time control function.
  • Explore the night sky with Night Vision mode, and protect user's eyesight in dark conditions.
  • Learn the history, mythology, and science of the sky with hundreds of captions about SkyPortal's objects.
  • Browse hundreds of astronomy and spacecraft photos from NASA.
  • Access over 4 hours of verbal narration to guide users on the best celestial objects.
