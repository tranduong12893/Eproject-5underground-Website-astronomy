A

lunar eclipse is an astronomical phenomenon when the Moon enters the shadow of the Earth, facing the Sun. This can only happen when the Sun, Earth, and Moon are aligned or approximately aligned, with the Earth in the middle. Therefore, lunar eclipses can only happen on full moon days. The type and length of a lunar eclipse depends on the position of the Moon relative to its orbital nodes.

A total lunar eclipse occurs when direct sunlight is completely obscured by the shadow of the Earth. The only visible light is refracted through the darkness of the Earth. This light is red for the same reason the sunset is red, due to Rayleigh scattering of shorter wavelength colored rays. Because of its red color, a total lunar eclipse is sometimes referred to as the blood moon.

Unlike an eclipse, which can only be seen from a relatively small area of the world, a lunar eclipse can be seen from anywhere in the dark half of the Earth. A lunar eclipse lasts for several hours, while a total eclipse lasts only for a few minutes at any position due to the smaller size of the Moon's shadow. Unlike an eclipse, a lunar eclipse can be safely observed with the naked eye because the eclipse image is blurred compared to the full moon image.

Maximum time of total lunar eclipse: 104 minutes (frequent recurrence); Partial lunar eclipse: 6 hours.

Types of lunar eclipses

T

hree main types of lunar eclipse:

A total lunar eclipse occurs when the Moon enters the shadow of the Earth. The moonlight will be dimmed at this point and the Moon will be bronze red or dark orange.

A partial lunar eclipse occurs when the Sun, Earth and Moon are in a near straight line. At this point the moonlight will be dimmed and the Moon is partially lost. The shadow of the black (or dark red) Earth can be seen obscuring the Moon. During a total lunar eclipse, a partial lunar eclipse can appear before and after a total lunar eclipse.

A semi-dark eclipse occurs when the Moon passes through the semi-dark region of the Earth. At this point the moonlight will dim and the Moon will dim and darken.

Lunar eclipse Selenelion

S

elenelion or selenehelion occurs when the Moon is obscured and the Sun can be observed for a while. This only happens before sunset or after dawn, and the two will appear together in opposing positions in the sky, near the horizon; that is when a lunar eclipse occurs when the sun has just risen or is about to set. This arrangement leads to the so-called horizon heaven eclipse.
