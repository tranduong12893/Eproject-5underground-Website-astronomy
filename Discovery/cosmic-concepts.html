Black Hole

A black hole also known as a black hole or black hole, is a region of space-time that has such a strong gravitational field that no matter in general occupies certain mass and space or what radiation and light can. escape. General relativity predicts that a sufficiently large amount of matter in a small enough range will deform spacetime to become a black hole.

The Comet

Comet is an asteroid-like object that is not made up of much rock, but mostly ice. It is described by some experts by the phrase "dirty snowball" because it contains carbon dioxide, methane and water that freezes with dirt and quality. Most elliptical orbits are very flattened, with some orbiting points much further away than Pluto.

The Galaxy

The Milky Way, the Milky Way, or the Milky Way, is a galaxy that contains our Solar System, appearing in the sky as a faint band of light extending from the constellation Cassiopeia. In the north to the constellation of the Southern Crux (Crux) to the south, and brightest in the constellation Sagittarius - the center of the Milky Way, the reason the Milky Way is called the Milky Way is because of god Zeus took his son, Hercules, and gave him the milk of the goddess Hera to become immortal. Suddenly the goddess woke up and the milk was splashed into the sky, from which the name Milky Way was born.

The Eclipse

An eclipse is a phenomenon that occurs when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun on the same line and observed from the Earth, at which time the Moon completely or partially obscures the Sun. This can only happen at the time of the new moon when viewed from the Earth, when the Sun is obscured by the Moon and the Moon's shadow overlaps the Earth. During a total solar eclipse, the sun disc was completely obscured. With a partial or annular eclipse, the solar disc is only partially obscured.

The lunar eclipse

A lunar eclipse is an astronomical phenomenon when the Moon enters the shadow of the Earth, facing the Sun. This can only happen when the Sun, Earth, and Moon are aligned or approximately aligned, with the Earth in the middle. Therefore, lunar eclipses can only happen on full moon days. The type and length of a lunar eclipse depends on the position of the Moon relative to its orbital nodes.
